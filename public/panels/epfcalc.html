🧓 EPS Pension — How It Works
▸Of your employer's 12% contribution, only ₹1,250/mo (8.33% of ₹15,000 wage ceiling) goes to the EPS pension fund — the rest goes to your EPF balance.
▸Pension formula: Monthly Pension = (Pensionable Salary × Service Years) ÷ 70, where Pensionable Salary = min(Basic, ₹15,000) and max service counted is 35 years.
▸Example: ₹15,000 × 30 years ÷ 70 = ~₹6,428/mo pension. Pension is not tax-free — it is taxed at your slab rate after retirement.
▸EPS contributions are not included in the EPF balance above — they build a separate pension entitlement payable for life.
⚠️ EPF Withdrawal — Tax Rules
✗Before 5 years of continuous service: Entire EPF withdrawal is taxable as per your income-tax slab in the year of withdrawal. TDS is deducted at 10% (u/s 192A) if withdrawal exceeds ₹50,000. No PAN? TDS jumps to 30%.
✓After 5 years of continuous service: EPF withdrawal is fully tax-free (Sec 10(12)). No TDS, no income-tax liability — whether withdrawn at retirement or before.
▸Key decision: If you're switching jobs before 5 years, do not withdraw EPF — transfer it via EPFO's online portal (Form 13) to preserve tax exemption and compound interest.
▸EPS (pension) withdrawal before 10 years of service gives a lump-sum; after 10 years you must opt for monthly pension or defer to 58.
