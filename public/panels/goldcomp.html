⏱️ Breakeven — When Physical Gold Catches Up
📊 Gold ETF — Tax Rule
Held >1 year → LTCG at 12.5% (no exemption limit). Held ≤1 year → Slab rate. Treated as equity for tax since Finance Act 2023. Cheapest regulated gold option. Minimum investment: 1 unit ≈ ½ gram gold price. Buy/sell on NSE/BSE during market hours.
🏦 Gold MF — Who Should Use
Gold Fund of Funds invest in Gold ETFs but don't need demat. SIP from ₹500/mo. Taxed at slab rate (not 12.5%) for both short and long term — disadvantage vs ETF for 20–30% slab taxpayers. Ideal for: Tier 2/3 towns, Jan Dhan users, first-time investors without demat accounts.
📿 Physical Gold — Real Cost
3% GST is added at purchase — so ₹1L investment is really ₹1.03L cost. Making charges 8–25% on jewellery (2–5% on coins). Locker: ₹1,500–₹5,000/yr. LTCG after 2 years at 20% with indexation (Finance Act 2023 removed indexation for many assets but physical gold retained it). Always buy BIS Hallmarked (916/750) gold only.
🏛️ Gold Monetisation Scheme (GMS)
Indian households hold ~25,000 tonnes of idle gold in lockers earning 0%. GMS lets you deposit idle jewellery at designated banks and earn 2–2.5% annual interest, tax-free. Minimum: 10 grams. Gold returned in equivalent gold or cash at maturity. Before Dhanteras/Akshaya Tritiya, check if your idle jewellery could be earning instead of sitting in a locker.
📋
Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) — Footnote Only
The government discontinued new SGB issuances after February 2024 with no announced plans to resume. The 2026 Union Budget also clarified that the capital gains exemption applies only to original subscribers holding to maturity — not to secondary market buyers on NSE/BSE. If you hold existing SGBs, continue holding to maturity (8 years) for the tax benefit. Do not buy SGBs on the secondary market expecting tax-free gains.
