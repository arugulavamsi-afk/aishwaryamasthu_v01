📖 What is Coffee Can Investing?

Coined by Robert Kirby (1984) and popularised in India by Saurabh Mukherjea (Marcellus Investment Managers) in Coffee Can Investing: The Low Risk Road to Stupendous Wealth (Mukherjea, Ranjan & Uniyal, 2018): lock high-quality businesses in a "coffee can" and forget them for 10 years. The philosophy demands companies with ≥10% revenue CAGR, ≥15% ROCE consistently over a decade, negligible debt, positive free cash flow every year, and stable promoter holdings — moat businesses that compound wealth without active management.

📉 Why Quality Stocks Still Fail
  • 1.Moat erosion: A ROCE of 40% today attracts competition. Entrants chip away pricing power — often invisibly until margin compression hits reported numbers 3–5 years later.
  • 2.Valuation entry price: Buying at 70–80× earnings means even flawless execution for 10 years may still deliver 8–10% CAGR — worse than a Nifty index fund bought at reasonable valuations.
  • 3.Regulatory & technology disruption: Pharma NLEM price controls, IT disruption by AI, paint sector disruption by Grasim — policy or technology can structurally break a moat overnight.
  • 4.Survivorship bias in this very list: You are seeing stocks that qualify today. Stocks that qualified in 2014 and subsequently failed (e.g. Relaxo post-FY23, Bajaj Consumer Care, Mayur Uniquoters) are not shown here.
  • 5.Promoter risk: Stable promoter pledging, undisclosed related-party transactions, and management ego-driven diversification have destroyed quality businesses in India (Eveready, Wockhardt, IL&FS).
✅ Why the Strategy Still Makes Sense
  • →The 70% that succeed compound so powerfully (20–35% CAGR over a decade) that they more than offset the 30% that disappoint — but only if you hold a basket, not a single name.
  • →Portfolio rule: Saurabh Mukherjea recommends 10–15 stocks minimum. Concentrating in 2–3 names from this list collapses the statistical advantage entirely.
  • →Behaviour edge: The real alpha is refusing to sell in panic. Studies show Coffee Can investors who held through 2008, 2013, 2020 downturns recovered and then compounded — those who traded did not.
  • →Annual review matters: Mukherjea explicitly re-screens every year. A stock that slips below ROCE 15% or shows FCF stress must exit — "coffee can" does not mean "never look again".

💡

The honest verdict: Coffee Can investing works at the portfolio level, not the individual stock level. Use this screener to build a diversified basket of 10–15 names, re-screen annually, and accept that roughly 3 in 10 picks will disappoint. The strategy's edge is the remaining 7 compounding undisturbed — not stock-picking perfection.
