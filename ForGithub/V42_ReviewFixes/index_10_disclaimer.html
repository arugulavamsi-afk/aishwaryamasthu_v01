Always Compare Against the Right Benchmark

A Nifty 50 Index Fund should be compared to Nifty 50 — not a smaller index. A fund adding 2% Alpha over its benchmark is excellent. But if a fund picks a weak benchmark to inflate numbers, the metrics look misleadingly good. Always check the benchmark listed in the fund's factsheet before trusting any metric.

Large Cap → Nifty 50 / Nifty 100 Mid Cap → Nifty Midcap 150 Flexi Cap → Nifty 500 Debt → CRISIL Composite Bond

⚠️ Benchmark Methodology — Please Read

What we use: Alpha, Beta, Sharpe, and Sortino are calculated against a category-appropriate index fund NAV (e.g., Nippon Nifty 100 for Large Cap, Motilal Nifty Midcap 150 for Mid Cap). We use fund NAVs because the AMFI data API does not expose raw index levels directly.

Why this matters: Every fund has a SEBI-mandated benchmark that may differ from our category proxy. A fund benchmarked to Nifty 50 inside the Large Cap bucket will show a different alpha vs Nifty 100. The proxy introduces a small tracking error (typically ±0.05–0.30% p.a.) that can shift alpha by ±0.1–0.5%.

Most reliable metrics: Rolling Hit Rate and Rolling Avg Return are benchmark-independent — they measure a fund's absolute consistency and are the most reliable signals here. CAGR and Std Dev are also unaffected by benchmark choice.

Before investing: Always verify Alpha and Sharpe against the fund's own factsheet (benchmarked to its SEBI-mandated index). This tool is for relative screening and comparison — not a substitute for the fund's official performance disclosure.

₿ Crypto / VDA Taxation — Budget 2022 Rules
30%
Flat Tax on ALL Gains
No holding period benefit. Gains taxed at 30% regardless of short-term or long-term. No basic exemption limit applies to VDA income.
1% TDS
Deducted on Every Trade
1% TDS deducted at source on every crypto transaction above ₹10,000 (₹50,000 for specified persons). TDS can be claimed as credit while filing.
No Offset
Losses Cannot Be Set Off
Crypto losses cannot be set off against any other income — not even other crypto gains. Each VDA is treated separately. No carry-forward of losses either.

Example: You bought Bitcoin at ₹30L and sold at ₹45L — gain of ₹15L. Tax = ₹4,50,000 (30%). Plus 1% TDS = ₹45,000 deducted by the exchange. If you separately lost ₹5L on Ethereum, that loss cannot reduce your Bitcoin tax. This is fundamentally different from equity — factor this into any crypto allocation decision.
